Ride the Best Waves in the Channel Islands

Guernsey's most popular west coast beach, famous for consistent surf, golden sand, and spectacular sunsets over the Atlantic.

About Vazon Beach

Vazon Beach is the beating heart of Guernsey's surf scene. When the Atlantic swells roll in, you'll find surfers of all levels paddling out to catch the island's most reliable waves. The beach faces west, making it perfectly positioned to receive swell from the open ocean.

The sandy beach stretches for nearly a mile, providing plenty of space for families, sunbathers, and water sports enthusiasts. At low tide, the exposed sand reveals rock pools teeming with marine life - perfect for curious children and wildlife spotters.

Surf Conditions

Vazon works best on a mid to high tide with westerly or north-westerly swells. The waves here are generally mellower than UK mainland spots, making it ideal for beginners and longboarders. Local surf schools operate from the beach during summer months.

Sunset Spot

As the day winds down, Vazon transforms into Guernsey's premier sunset viewing location. The sun dips directly into the Atlantic horizon, painting the sky in brilliant oranges and purples. Bring a camera - this is Instagram gold.

Visitor Tips

  • Check surf forecast before visiting - Magic Seaweed covers Vazon
  • Weekend mornings are busiest with local surfers
  • The left side of the beach (facing sea) is best for beginners
  • Cafe closes at 5pm - bring supplies for sunset watching
  • Wetsuit recommended even in summer - Atlantic water is chilly
